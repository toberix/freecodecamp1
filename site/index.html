Olusegun Okikiola Obasanjo, GCFR

The man with BIG heart

When former president Olusegun Obasanjo, paid a visit to United Nations Headquarters on 13th October 1977 to meet with Secretary-General Kurt Waldheim, and to address the General Assembly.

Here's a time line of Olusegun Okikiola Obasanjo's life:

  • 1937 - Born in Owu, Abeokuta
  • 1958 - He enlisted in the Nigerian Army. He attended the 6-month Short Service Commission training at Mons Officer Cadet School in Aldershot in England, and was thereafter commissioned as an officer in the Nigerian Army.
  • 1975 - The military regimes of Murtala and Obasanjo benefited from oil revenues that increased 350 percent between
  • 1979 - Obasanjo handed power to Shehu Shagari, a democratically elected civilian president
  • 1983 - The military seized power, Gen. Buhari and Gen. Idiagbon took over
  • 1985 - Gen. Babangida seized power from Buhari
  • 1999 - Obasanjo decided to run for the presidency as the candidate of the People's Democratic Party (PDP). Obasanjo won with 62.6% of the vote
  • 2003 Obasanjo was re-elected, also gave asylum to the deposed Liberian president, Charles Taylor.
  • 2010 - He became chairman of the PDP Board of Trustees, with control over nominations for governmental positions and even policy and strategy.
  • 2012 - He voluntary resigned as the chairman board of trustees of the PD
  • 2014 - Obasanjo wrote to President Goodluck Jonathan requesting that he should mediate on behalf of the Nigerian government for the release of the Chibok girls held by the Boko Haram militants.
  • 2015 - he quit the ruling party and directed a PDP ward leader to tear his membership card during a press conference.
  • 2017 - Celebrated his birthday in a well glamorous way.

"And our distinguished guest this morning was not only a courageous fighter for liberty and independence but, in his first visit to our own Nation, he came here as one seeking to reconstruct the economy and the people's lives of Nigeria. He's a distinguished military man in war and also has proven himself to be a distinguished military leader in peace."

Jimmy Carter
